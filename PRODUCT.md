# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Solo content creators — bloggers, experts, and SMM specialists working with short vertical video (YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels, TikTok). They use Viral during focused work sessions: hunting viral formats, saving references, and turning trends into shootable scripts. Primary market context is Russian-speaking creators (UI in Russian), often working alone without an agency toolchain.

## Product Purpose

Viral is a research-and-production dashboard for short-form video. It aggregates trending clips, competitor reels, saved references, and AI script generation in one calm workspace — so a creator can go from "what's working now?" to "here's my script" without tab-hopping across social platforms.

Success in the near term is measured by conversion: free and trial users clearly understand value, hit meaningful limits, and upgrade to paid tiers (PRO / BUSINESS) or buy token packs when they need more searches, transcriptions, or script generations.

## Brand Personality

Friendly guide — approachable, encouraging, and clear. Viral should feel like a capable teammate who respects the creator's time: warm without being childish, confident without being cold. Copy and UI lead with plain language and next steps, not jargon or hype.

Emotional goal: relief and momentum. The user should feel oriented (not overwhelmed by feeds) and nudged toward action (save, script, upgrade when it genuinely helps).

## Anti-references

- Noisy social apps — infinite-scroll chaos, loud colors, attention-bait UI, cluttered feeds that compete with the content.
- Generic SaaS dashboards — cream templates, purple gradient heroes, interchangeable metric cards, decorative glass for its own sake.
- AI-template aesthetics — gradient text, eyebrow kickers on every section, identical icon-card grids, side-stripe accent borders.

Viral surfaces video thumbnails and trend data; the chrome should stay quiet so the content stays primary.

## Design Principles

1. **Guide, don't shout** — Every screen answers "what can I do here?" in one glance. Hierarchy and copy do the work; decoration does not.
2. **Calm density** — Show rich data (scores, trends, competitors) without social-feed noise. Prefer scannable grids and sidebars over endless scroll traps.
3. **Content is the hero** — Thumbnails, titles, and scripts are the focal point; UI chrome recedes.
4. **Earn the upgrade** — Free and trial flows demonstrate real value before limits bite. Paywalls and token costs are transparent, not punitive.
5. **Respect the solo workflow** — Optimize for one person moving fast: few modals, recoverable actions, clear token/subscription state in account surfaces.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Sensible defaults: readable contrast on body text, keyboard-usable controls, and `prefers-reduced-motion` respected for animations. No formal WCAG certification target at this stage; avoid known failure modes (low-contrast gray body text, motion-only state changes).
